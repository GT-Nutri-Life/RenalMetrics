⚠️ Important Medical Disclaimer

This application provides educational estimates only. It is NOT a substitute for professional medical nutrition therapy. Calculations use evidence-based formulas (Mifflin-St Jeor, KDOQI 2020, ADA guidelines) but may vary ±10–15% from indirect calorimetry. Always consult a Registered Dietitian (RD) and/or nephrologist/endocrinologist for individualized plans. Lab values (eGFR, serum K⁺, PO₄, HbA1c, albumin) must guide clinical decisions. Never use this tool for direct medical management without professional oversight.

Clinical Resources & Guidelines
Evidence-based sources used in this tool's calculations

KDOQI 2020 – Nutrition in CKD

Comprehensive guidelines for energy (25–35 kcal/kg/day), protein by CKD stage, and electrolyte management. Primary reference for this tool's CKD calculations.

→ View Guideline

ADA Standards of Care – 2024

American Diabetes Association guidelines for nutrition therapy, carbohydrate management, insulin-to-carb ratios, and DKD protein recommendations (0.8 g/kg non-dialysis).

→ View Guideline

KDIGO – Diabetes in CKD 2022

International guidelines on managing DKD: protein targets, glycemic control, cardiovascular risk reduction, and dietary sodium restriction.

→ View Guideline

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

Preferred BMR formula (more accurate than Harris-Benedict in most populations). Men: (10×W)+(6.25×H)−(5×A)+5. Women: −161 instead of +5.

Primary BMR Formula Used

ESPEN – Enteral Nutrition

European Society for Clinical Nutrition guidelines for enteral (NG) feeding: formula selection, delivery rates, monitoring parameters for CKD/ICU patients.

→ View Guideline

USDA FoodData Central

Comprehensive food nutrient database. Values for protein, carbohydrate, fat, potassium, phosphorus, sodium used in this tool's food database.

→ Search Database

Key Formulas Used in This Tool
Mifflin-St Jeor BMR:
Males: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) – (5 × age) + 5
Females: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) – (5 × age) – 161
Devine IBW Formula:
Males: IBW = 50 + 2.3 × (height_in – 60)
Females: IBW = 45.5 + 2.3 × (height_in – 60)
Adjusted Body Weight (AjBW):
AjBW = IBW + 0.4 × (ABW – IBW) [for BMI > 30]
KDOQI 2020 Protein Recs:
CKD 3–5 (non-dialysis): 0.55–0.8 g/kg IBW/day
MHD: 1.0–1.2 g/kg/day | PD: 1.0–1.2 g/kg/day
DKD non-dialysis: 0.8 g/kg/day

Clinical Glossary
eGFR – Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate. Measure of kidney function. Normal: ≥90 mL/min/1.73m².
CKD – Chronic Kidney Disease. Staged 1–5 based on eGFR and proteinuria.
MHD – Maintenance Hemodialysis. Regular dialysis sessions (typically 3×/week).
PD – Peritoneal Dialysis. Dialysis using the peritoneal membrane.
PEW – Protein-Energy Wasting. Malnutrition state in CKD/dialysis patients.
DKD – Diabetic Kidney Disease. CKD caused by diabetes.
HBV Protein – High Biological Value protein (eggs, meat, fish). Contains all essential amino acids.
TDEE – Total Daily Energy Expenditure. BMR × activity factor.
IBW – Ideal Body Weight. Calculated via Devine formula.
HbA1c – Glycated hemoglobin. 3-month average blood glucose marker.
Hyperkalemia – High serum K⁺ (>5.0 mEq/L). Restrict dietary potassium.
Hyperphosphatemia – High serum PO₄ (>4.5 mg/dL). Restrict dietary phosphorus.
